How to Read Your Metabolic Panel: A Patient's Guide to the Numbers That Actually Matter

Dr. David Chen 2026-04-12 7 min read
Advertisement
Ad Space - 300x250
I review probably 40 metabolic panels a week. Most patients look at them like they're written in ancient Greek. "Doctor, is this good?" they ask, pointing at a page of numbers they don't understand. So I started giving them this guide. A simple breakdown of what each marker means, what the ranges are, and why they matter. Here it is, adapted for anyone who wants to understand their own labs. **Fasting Glucose** - Normal: <100 mg/dL - Pre-diabetes: 100-125 - Diabetes: ≥126 This is your blood sugar after 8+ hours without food. It reflects how well your pancreas is keeping up with insulin demand. Even at the high end of normal (95-99), you're at elevated risk. I like to see <90. **HbA1c** - Normal: <5.7% - Pre-diabetes: 5.7-6.4% - Diabetes: ≥6.5% A1c shows your average blood sugar over 2-3 months. It's more stable than fasting glucose — less affected by what you ate yesterday. If your fasting glucose is borderline, A1c helps clarify. Both should be checked. **Total Cholesterol** - Desirable: <200 mg/dL - Borderline: 200-239 - High: ≥240 Less useful than the breakdown below. I rarely focus on total cholesterol alone. **LDL Cholesterol ("Bad")** - Optimal: <100 - Near optimal: 100-129 - Borderline high: 130-159 - High: 160-189 - Very high: ≥190 The main driver of atherosclerosis. Lower is generally better, though extremely low (<50) may have risks we don't fully understand. For high-risk patients (diabetes, known heart disease), I target <70. **HDL Cholesterol ("Good")** - Low (bad): <40 men, <50 women - Acceptable: 40-59 - High (protective): ≥60 HDL carries cholesterol away from arteries. Higher is better. Low HDL is an independent risk factor even if everything else is normal. **Triglycerides** - Normal: <150 - Borderline: 150-199 - High: 200-499 - Very high: ≥500

Reflects how your body processes fat and sugar. Elevated triglycerides often go with low HDL and insulin resistance. Very high (>500) risks pancreatitis. **Blood Pressure** - Normal: <120/80 - Elevated: 120-129/<80 - Stage 1 HTN: 130-139 or 80-89 - Stage 2 HTN: ≥140 or ≥90 The silent killer. No symptoms until damage is done. Check it regularly, even if you feel fine. **ALT (Liver Enzyme)** - Normal: <40 U/L (varies by lab) Elevated ALT suggests fatty liver, which is increasingly common and often linked to insulin resistance. Even mild elevation (40-60) deserves attention. **The pattern I watch for:** Metabolic syndrome is diagnosed by 3+ of: 1. Waist >40in men / >35in women 2. Triglycerides ≥150 3. HDL <40 men / <50 women 4. BP ≥130/85 5. Fasting glucose ≥100 If you have 2, you're on the path. If you have 3+, you're there. The good news: metabolic syndrome is reversible with 5-10% weight loss, increased activity, and dietary changes. **What to ask your doctor:** "Can you explain my metabolic panel in plain English?" "Which of these numbers concern you most?" "What's my trend over time?" "Should I be checking any of these at home?" A good doctor will walk you through each number and explain what it means for you specifically. If they just say "everything looks fine" without details, ask for specifics. It's your body. You deserve to understand it. Dr. David Chen P.S. I check my own metabolic panel every 6 months. Last one: glucose 90, A1C 5.2, LDL 112, HDL 48, triglycerides 95, BP 116/74. Not perfect — HDL could be higher — but solid. Track your trends, not single readings.
