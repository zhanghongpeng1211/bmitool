BMI Is Just a Screening Tool

BMI has serious limitations. It cannot tell the difference between muscle and fat. It does not account for age, ethnicity, bone density, or where you carry your weight. A bodybuilder and a couch potato can have the same BMI.

Always confirm your BMI results with a healthcare provider who can look at the full picture: blood work, body composition, family history, and lifestyle.

When to See a Doctor

  • Your BMI is under 18.5 or over 30
  • You are pregnant, nursing, or planning pregnancy
  • You have a medical condition (diabetes, thyroid issues, heart disease, etc.)
  • You are under 18 or over 65 (BMI ranges differ)
  • You are an athlete or have high muscle mass
  • You are experiencing unexplained weight loss or gain
