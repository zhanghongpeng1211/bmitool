Why I Built BMITool.org

I built this site because I was tired of seeing patients come to my clinic with misinformation from oversimplified online calculators. A BMI of 24.2 with a "normal" label while fasting glucose is 118. A parent panicking because an adult BMI calculator called their 8-year-old "obese." An Asian patient with a BMI of 23.4 being told they're "fine" while their metabolic panel says otherwise.

These calculators are free because health information should be accessible. They're clinical-grade because patients deserve accuracy. And they're transparent about limitations because no single number defines your health.

Clinical Philosophy

I don't believe in "ideal weight." I believe in metabolic health. I don't celebrate rapid weight loss. I emphasize sustainable behavior change. I don't use BMI as a diagnostic. I use it as one screening tool among many.

My approach combines population-level data with individual context: ethnicity, age, sex, body composition, family history, and metabolic markers. The calculators on this site reflect that approach. They're not perfect — no calculator is. But they're closer to clinical reality than anything you'll find on WebMD.
